Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar, observed by Muslims worldwide as a month of fasting, prayer, reflection, and community. It is considered one of the Five Pillars of Islam and is a time for spiritual growth and self-discipline.

During Ramadan, Muslims fast from dawn until sunset, refraining from eating, drinking, smoking, and engaging in sinful behavior. The fast is broken each day with a meal called iftar, which often includes dates and water, followed by a larger meal. The pre-dawn meal before the fast begins is called suhoor.

Ramadan is also a time for increased prayer and recitation of the Quran. Many Muslims attend special nightly prayers called Tarawih at the mosque. The month culminates in the celebration of Eid al-Fitr, a festive holiday marking the end of Ramadan, where families and friends gather to share meals and exchange gifts.
